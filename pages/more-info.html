What is AQI?

AQI is a standardized way to communicate air quality to the public. It transforms complex air quality data into simple numbers and colors.

Pollutants Measured

PM2.5, PM10, Ozone, Carbon Monoxide, Sulfur Dioxide, and Nitrogen Dioxide are the main pollutants tracked.

How It's Calculated

AQI is calculated based on the highest concentration of any single pollutant using EPA-approved formulas.

Why Air Quality Matters

Understanding the health impacts of air pollution

Lung Health

  • Causes respiratory irritation
  • Worsens asthma symptoms
  • Reduces lung function
  • Increases infection risk

Heart Health

  • Increases blood pressure
  • Higher heart attack risk
  • Irregular heart rhythm
  • Stroke risk elevation

Sensitive Groups

  • Children under 14
  • Adults over 65
  • Pregnant women
  • People with chronic conditions
